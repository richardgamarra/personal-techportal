Practice Strategy

How to use practice exams correctly

  • Do not memorize answers.
  • Track weak domains after every test.
  • Review every missed question in your own words.
  • Repeat only after re-studying the domain.
  • Save one fresh timed exam for the final week.
Use practice exams as a diagnosis tool, not just a score tool.

🧭 Recommended Study Strategy

🚀
Beginner Path

Best study order for beginners

  1. Read the official exam page and objectives.
  2. Watch a full free course from Professor Messer.
  3. Take notes by domain, not by video order.
  4. Use flashcards daily for acronyms and core terms.
  5. Start practice exams only after first full content pass.
  6. Re-study weak domains, then re-test.
🎯
Pass Strategy

How to maximize passing odds

  • Know the objectives better than the resource names.
  • Focus on concepts, not memorized answer patterns.
  • Treat PBQs as applied troubleshooting questions.
  • Practice reading answers carefully and eliminating distractors.
  • Schedule the exam only after consistent practice scores and objective coverage.
Consistency beats resource hoarding.
📅
Sample Plan

4-week sample study plan

Week 1: Objectives + video course start
Week 2: Finish course + build notes
Week 3: Practice exams + weak domains
Week 4: Cram review + flashcards + one final timed exam

Stretch it longer if you are new to networking, cryptography, or security operations.

Final Checklist

Before you schedule the exam

  • You have reviewed all official objectives.
  • You can explain, not just recognize, key concepts.
  • You have done at least one full timed practice exam.
  • You know your weakest domain and have reviewed it twice.
  • You understand the testing process and ID requirements.
